CLIENT   National Railway Museum, Taipei

DIGITAL   IF Plus Art
YEAR   2025
TYPE   Permanent exhibition
FORMAT   Permanent brand experience installation

A permanent exhibition staging the invisible: how energy flows through a 100-year-old locomotive repair hall.


For the S212 Diesel-Electric locomotive, a large-format transparent screen sits in front of the actual machinery, animating the flow of electric power while keeping the physical mechanisms visible behind — an X-ray overlay aligning virtual animation to real hardware.

For the armature maintenance area, a full-scale 60 kg armature model is choreographed with the overhead crane, replaying the actual motion sequence of factory workflow.

Commercial · 04

AI Living Lab

MODA · 2025 資訊月 · 台北
CLIENT   Ministry of Digital Affairs (MODA)
VENUE   Taipei Computer Festival 2025
STUDIO   IF Plus Art
AUDIENCE   1,000+ participants
DURATION   4 days
YEAR   2025
TYPE   Government digital affairs activation
Fig. 01 — Interactive cycling station, MODA booth

Pedal, choose a route, answer a prompt. The system generates a one-of-a-kind audio souvenir — cassette postcard — from your ride.

Visitors mounted a custom-built stationary bike and turned the handlebars to choose a virtual route, answering short prompts about how AI shows up in daily life. Pedaling speed, route choices, and prompt responses streamed in real time to an AI music-generation model I developed and trained specifically for this project.

At the end of each ride, the system printed a one-of-a-kind cassette postcard — an audio souvenir generated uniquely by the rider's physical and semantic input. Over four days, more than 1,000 visitors rode the bike, producing everything from aggressive electronic tracks to ambient loops.

My role

AI music model development & training · pedaling sensor data acquisition & processing · real-time program integration · custom bike hardware design.

07 — Research · 01 Series · 3 Versions · 2022 — 2024
Research · 01

Threshold

闁 · Electromechanical Neural Audio Series

A three-version series examining recursive feedback between real-time neural audio synthesis and physical resonance systems.

v1 · Taichung 2022

Site-specific

Collected and algorithmically transformed physical space vibration and environmental audio in real time.

v2 · C-LAB Taipei 2023

Latent Space

Modified RAVE model for real-time latent-space audio reconstruction across a 49.4-channel Ambisonics system — first deployment of real-time neural audio at this scale in Taiwan's institutional sound-art context.

v3 · NYCU 2024

Electromechanical

Gate-shaped apparatus: brass plate driven by stepper motors, solenoids, and pneumatics forms a continuous feedback loop with RAVE synthesis; k-means redistributes output into a five-voice generative score.

My role — solo

Composition · modified RAVE pipeline · electromechanical apparatus design · Ambisonics mapping · feedback-loop engineering across all three versions.

08 — Research · 02 Digital Art Center Taipei
Research · 02

Circle of Confusion — Reaction

彌散圈反應 · VR Installation · 2024

A VR installation exploring the photographic notion of the "circle of confusion" — mediation, diffusion, blur, process.

Music triggers cascading algorithmic transformations inside a non-representational VR environment. A custom 3D-printed sculpture attached to the VR headset translates virtual image into tactile sensation.

The spatial audio uses a deliberately incomplete Ambisonics array — speaker absences become bidirectional "channels," thresholds through which the viewer becomes self-aware of their body against the technical field. A CO₂ / O₂ gas system gives the installation a respiratory rhythm.

09 — Research · 03 NYCU · iOS AR
Research · 03

River of Shadows

川影 · Site & Time-Specific iOS AR · 2024

A site-specific, time-specific iOS AR performance. At a designated moment, audiences open the app inside the ground floor of the university's administrative building.


Area Target spatial recognition activates the performance from a first-person perspective. The work superimposes two absent spaces onto the physical corridor — the artist's private room and the desert pavilion of Taichung Botanical Garden. The room functions as a negative space — a chroma-key cut-out subtracted from the visible field, producing a ruptured scene. A moving white cube becomes a navigation score, directing each viewer's physical movement through the corridor in sync with spatial electroacoustic composition.

My role — solo

iOS AR app development · Area Target spatial recognition · spatial electroacoustic composition · audience navigation choreography.

13-Voice CV Synthesizer

十三聲部電壓控制合成器 · Analog Circuit Design
TYPE   Solo hardware instrument
ARCHITECTURE   1 master board · 12 sub-boards
CONTROL   Full CV — voltage-controlled
VOICES   13
PCB   Self-designed · hand-fabricated
Fig. 01 — Assembled · master board and 12 sub-boards

A fully self-designed, self-fabricated analog synthesizer. One master voice and twelve sub-voices, each independently CV-controlled through a shared voltage bus.

PCB layouts designed from scratch and hand-etched. The architecture separates a master board from twelve identical sub-boards, enabling full polyphonic or multitimbral control from a single CV source. Built as a proof of analog circuit design capability at system scale.

11 — Current · In Development Submitted · ZKM Karlsruhe 2026/27
Current · In Development

LATENT BREACH

Adversarial Subspaces as Performative Resistance

A 20-minute live performance in which every audience member's smartphone becomes a sensing instrument.

An iOS / Android AR app runs real-time YOLO26 object detection while six UWB beacons track each device to centimeter-level precision. For the first ten minutes, the system classifies the room accurately — building trust.

At minute ten, PGD adversarial perturbation collapses the detection confidence, and an AR entity materializes in the shared airspace. Object labels are replaced by site-specific narrative text authored from archival research. The machine does not malfunction. It narrates.
